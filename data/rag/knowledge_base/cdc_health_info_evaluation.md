# CDC / health-literacy — evaluating health information online (high-level)

## Why it matters
Health claims are a common misinformation vector. Basic evaluation heuristics reduce harm when the content is uncertain.

## Evaluation checklist (summary)
- **Identify the source**: who publishes the page (institution / agency / author)?
- **Prefer authoritative domains** for public health guidance (e.g., `.gov`, national agencies, WHO).
- **Check the date**: health guidance changes; stale content can mislead.
- **Look for evidence**: credible medical claims should be consistent with scientific consensus and cite studies/guidance.
- **Cross-check**: compare across multiple trusted sources rather than relying on a single post/site.
