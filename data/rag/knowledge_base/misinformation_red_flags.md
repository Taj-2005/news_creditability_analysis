# Misinformation red flags — pattern checklist

## Linguistic / framing patterns
- **“Miracle cure” / “one weird trick”** framing with absolute certainty.
- **Urgency + virality**: “share before deleted”, “they don’t want you to know”.
- **Overclaiming evidence**: “scientists proved” without naming a study, journal, or institution.
- **Conspiracy scaffolding**: “cover-up”, “mainstream media won’t report”.
- **Anonymous authority**: “a doctor said”, “an insider revealed” with no identity.

## Evidence patterns
- No primary source, no data, no reproducible reference.
- Screenshots of posts instead of original documents.
- Single source repeated across multiple low-quality sites.

## Safer handling
When signals are strong but evidence is absent, categorize as **high risk / low credibility** and mark key claims as **unknown** until corroborated.
