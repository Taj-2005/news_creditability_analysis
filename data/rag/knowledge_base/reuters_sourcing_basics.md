# Reuters — sourcing and attribution basics (journalistic standards)

## What it is
Reuters’ published standards emphasize **accuracy**, **transparent corrections**, and **honest sourcing**. This is useful as a credibility rubric: what would a careful newsroom require before stating a claim as fact?

## Practical sourcing rules (summary)
- **Prefer named sources** whenever possible; anonymity should be exceptional and justified.
- **Cross-check**: two independent sources are generally stronger than one.
- **Avoid misleading sourcing language**: do not imply verification you don’t have; be explicit about uncertainty.
- **Attribute clearly** when material is not your own (photos, video, claims from another outlet).
- **Resist “rumor-first” reporting**: distinguish fact vs rumor; explain why something is being reported (e.g., market impact) and what is verified.

## Signals of higher credibility
- Clear attribution (who said what, when, where, under what conditions).
- Primary evidence or direct observation, not “it was learned/announced” without a source.
- Transparent correction behavior (errors acknowledged promptly).
