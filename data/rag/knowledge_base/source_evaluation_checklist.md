# Source evaluation — quick rubric

## Identity & accountability
- Is there a **real author / organization** with a track record?
- Is there a clear **About / Contact** page and verifiable affiliation?
- Are claims **attributed** to named sources or primary documents?

## Evidence quality
- Does the article link to **primary documents** (official statements, datasets, court filings, peer‑reviewed papers)?
- Are statistics presented with denominators, time windows, and definitions?
- Are counterpoints or limitations acknowledged?

## Consistency checks
- Check the same claim across multiple independent outlets or official sources.
- Check time: is the claim **current** or recycled?

## Common failure modes
- Overreliance on unnamed sources, sensational headlines, or “handout” media without origin.
- Mixing commentary/opinion with factual claims without clear labeling.
