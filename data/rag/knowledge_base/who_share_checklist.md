# WHO — SHARE checklist (spot misinformation)

## What it is
The World Health Organization (WHO) proposes the **SHARE** checklist to help people slow down and evaluate content before sharing.

## Checklist (SHARE)
- **S — Source**: Prefer official / primary sources for health & safety information (e.g., WHO, national public health authorities).
- **H — Headline**: Headlines can mislead; read beyond the headline and scan the full article.
- **A — Analyse**: If a claim sounds unbelievable, treat it as suspicious; look for evidence and corroboration.
- **R — Retouched**: Images/videos can be edited or unrelated; verify context and re-use.
- **E — Error**: Typos, inconsistent formatting, and “off” presentation can be signals of low-quality or false content.

## When to mark as “unknown”
If the content is emotional or urgent but you cannot find a clear source or corroboration, treat it as **uncertain** rather than true/false.
